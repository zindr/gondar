Kenya

Nairobi (2006-01):

The city in Nairobi is as close as you can get to any big western city, at least in the center. Security improved a lot in most areas between 6AM and 9 PM, well dressed, polite and helpful people. The Nairobbery of before is illustrated by the many shops behind bars. I was fun.

Naivasha (2006-01):

This small town can be a base to visit Hell's gate, a crater lake and crescent island. The free part of crescent island was alright, and the town was not the worst.

Masai Mara (2006-01):

A 2 day safari in the NP made us see some lions, buffalos, cheetahs, elephants, giraffes, ... but I skipped on the crocs and hippos that took at least 3 days to include the pool.

Nakuru (2006-01):

A city in Kenya, not really interesting but still nice. It is the gateway to visit the nearby NP, if you can spare the money. I visited somewhat boring Lake Elementaita for 2 US instead.

Garissa (2006-01):

Change to get a bus, not a place to stay.

Wajir (2006-01):

Short stop on route, not a place to stay.

Moyale (2006-01):

A two country border town, we just took a ride on top of a cow transport truck from 14h to 10h... the trip of hell that I would not want to have missed... through the desert through the night.

Isiolo (2006-02):

Home of the colorful Turkana people, and other tribals. The town itself is not really interesting beyond that. I enjoyed being able to eat camel meat.

Malaba (2006-02):

For being a border town this town of Mabala was actually really OK. Again, nothing much to do though.
