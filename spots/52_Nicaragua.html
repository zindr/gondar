Nicaragua

Managua (2010-02):

Managua for me was again a brilliant show of the tension between rich and poor, I loved it for the few days I was there but I am sure not many others would. It is one of the few places that sometimes gets to be a little bit surreal.

Esteli (2017-04):

A nice city all in all, without being special in any way. It was nice to be back in Nicaragua.

Leon (2010-02):

A nice colonial city most tourists would enjoy. It has an amazing museum, do not miss it. A number of side trips are possible, for example surfboarding a vulcano.

Masaya (2017-04):

This is a popular place because of the nearby volcano, and it is alright as a city.

Matagalpa (2010-02):

Cowboy country in the highlands. It has some coffee farms nearby and some hikes are possible, even one in the jungle taking you about 3 hours.

Miraflor NP (2017-04):

There are some very nice stretches in this national park, dotted with tiny villages and houses. It was a good day hike.

Granada (2010-02):

A tourist city which is charming at first site, but quickly getting boring. 

Laguna De Apoyo (2010-02):

Just a crater lake in which you can have a swim if that is your thing.

Rivas (2010-02):

The port town towards the Isla de Omoteppe has a nice real feel to it but will not keep you there long.

Isla de Omotepe (2010-02):

This you can not miss, an island made of two merged volcano that you can climb. The island boasts a tranquil live despite the many tourists that visit is.

Bleufields (2010-02):

Caribian style Bleufields does not have much to do (yet) but still is really nice to hang out because everything here is just a bit different. To get around boats are typically used.

Laguna Las Perlas (2010-02):

A smaller place is the Laguna Las Perlas where you can enjoy visiting the canals and small islands and swimming. Nothing much here to keep you around though.

San Carlos (2010-02):

For a port border town this one is fairly normal and OK, but still most people would leave the day after.

El Castillo de La Concepcion (2010-02):

The boat trip through the jungle to get there is more than half the fun. The castle is worth a visit, while the town probably is not. Nearby towns may be nicer.
