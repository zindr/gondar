Morocco

Rabat (2007-11):

Another modern city in contemporary Morocco. The new part has a fresh feel to it, and the old Medina is also nice to hang out... and eat fish kebabs. The tomb is enjoyable, as is the walk up there...

Casablanca (2007-11):

A modern city in the north of Africa, I only visited it very briefly but it seemed OK. The Medina at night with a backpack may have been stretching it a bit, but most people were very nice, surprisingly. The huge mosque comes with a huge ticket price too...

Fes (2007-12):

Here you come to enjoy the Medina, a twirling set of small streets on a hill... very enchanting, and a real taste of old day Morocco city life.

Marrakesh (2007-12):

A beautiful, big and lively square with snakes on music, snails for a snack, 40 stands to sell orange juice... but maybe a bit too touristy.

Essaouira (2007-12):

The beach in a touristy and relaxed town. The rough sea pounding the city walls is a nice sight. OK for trying out some food.

Ouarzazate (2007-12):

Brilliant surroundings... and a bit of a peculiar atmosphere kinda town. I have not been there more than a number of hours... There is a big film-studio quite near, apparently.

Taroudant (2007-12):

short stop over in this OK city, very normal looking given that it is Morocco. 

Tiznit (2007-12):

Just a city, nothing of interest in particular and not particularly charming... but still OK and nice to get into contact with ordinary life.

Tafraoute (2007-12):

In the mountains, this is nice for walking... the beautiful hill-side can entertain you for the day. The town itself, although enchanting, is smallish and has nothing much of particular interest.

Walk Tafraoute (2007-12):

The walk in the beautiful area was nice.

Dakhla (2007-12):

A beach peninsula, arriving there is brilliant as you drive a sandy plain across the ocean. It is the last stop before Mauritania. For kite lovers, there are many.
