Mali

Bamako (2007-12):

The capital has a nice typical African city feel to it. Low rise buildings with lots of activity on the streets, a lot of sandy roads and dust. There are some markets of which one sells everything going from monkey penises to human skulls.... voodoo people.

Kayes (2007-12):

Nothing of particular interest in this town, although it is OK for being a day in between Senegal and Bamako.

Sevare (2008-01):

A nearby alternative to Mopti, a bit less over touristed.

Mopti (2008-01):

The Venice of Africa is where you take the boat to Timbuktu. For the boat to Timbuktu I paid 12.5 Euro after bargaining really hard (and friendly), while other tourists on the same boat paid 25 Euro and 50 Euro for the same thing.

Kouriome (2008-01):

Avoid it as much as you can. It is poor, people have nothing to do and they know tourists. 

Timbuktu (2008-01):

Timbuktu is a name, it is a myth... it is also an ordinary dusty sandy town near the desert. I am happy the boat trip up there was so brilliant otherwise I may have been dissappointed, but nevertheless, it is Timbuktu and I have been there.

Bandiagara (2008-01):

The small town of Bandiagara, at the verge of the Dogon Valley, is kinda nice and OK for hanging out a day although having nothing of interest.

Dogon Valley (2008-01):

The valley is a story in itself, and a highlight in Mali if you love nature or culture. But, prepare yourself for a touristed crappy few days. I enjoyed my rush through trip, especially because of the scenery and the walk (without guide, although many people say that it is impossible).

Hike Dogon Valley (2008-01):

A two day hike through the valley on my own ended up being really nice.
