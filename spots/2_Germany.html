Germany

Oberstdorf (1980-00):

This little town near the Bodensee I visited when I was a kid.

Eifel (2013-12):

The Eifel is an OK place for some simple walks through forest, offering a bit of WWII history.

Monschau (2013-12):

Historic town that draws from tourism, nice to walk through.

Gemund (2013-12):

sleepover, town

Eushkirchen (2013-12):

stopover, city

Berlin (2014-05):

Very nice city, with a great vibe, a lot of parks and that little bit of roughness. I loved it, and the party people will probably love it too.

Heidelberg (2015-07):

The university city of Heidelberg is maybe one of the nicer stops on route to the south. You might want to visit the castle uphill.

Rothenburg ob der Tauber (2021-07):

Interesting city wall, and perfect stop in a road trip. This small city is one of Germany's most famous.

Nuremberg (2021-07):

This famous city is nice to have a break in, did not spent much time though.

Bad Schandau (2021-07):

The start of our version of the Malerweg hike, and a break in the middle as well. It is a touristy town.

Bastei (2021-07):

Main attraction of part one of our Malerweg hike, the Bastei bridge.

Schrammsteine (2021-07):

Main attraction of part two of our Malerweg hike, the Schrammsteine Tor, a huge sandstone rock in the middle of many more.

Hinterdittersbach (2021-07):

Main attraction of part four of our Malerweg hike, the path towards to border with Czechia.

Dresden (2021-07):

Definitely worth a stop, the altstadt, with monumental buildings and a bit of the roughness I liked most of Berlin.

Leipzig (2021-07):

The new hipster hot spot it seems, lovely industrial bars and restaurants, and a lot of vegan and surprisingly also Vietnamese.
