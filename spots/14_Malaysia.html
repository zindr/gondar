Malaysia

Kuala Lumpur (2013-04):

The capital is quite nice with its diversity, but many tourist attractions there are not and it is a busy place to try and get around. Off course, the second time I was there, during Covid-19 lockdown, getting around was easy when you were still alowed to go out at least.

Melaka (2001-09):

This colonial city has some interesting buildings but mainly it has a port towards Indonesia. It is nice to walk around because of its mix of colonial influences.

Georgetown (2001-10):

Stopover, for taking the train to Bangkok

Penang (2001-10):

Another nice colonial city with some remarkable colonial buildings and an interesting Indian and Chinese area.

Kota Bharu (2020-03):

This city is a fine place to enter Malaysia, not much to do but relaxed and all the amenities are there. From here you can take the train south to the jungle if you want.

Jerantut (2020-03):

The gateway to the Taman Negara National Park appears OK, bordering the jungle. I just went in and out to get to the park.

Taman Negara NP (2020-03):

Bordering tiny Kuala Tahan the oldest and most famous national park of Malaysia can be found, a fun place to hike for a few days. You can DIY but it is not the most adventurous hike, altough you might run into a Tapir if you are more lucky than I was.
