Costa Rica

San Jose (2010-03):

Looking modern at the main street but much rougher a bit further. It is again another slightly dodgy but interesting place in where there is not much to do.

Monteverde (2010-02):

A tourist heaven; hike superb cloud forest, slide the zip-line through jungle, do a night safari to spot tarantulas. Join the drunk 20 year old tourist in the afternoon on the street... crap.

Upala (2010-02):

If you want to see what a real city without tourists would be like, this may do it... very friendly and boring.

Puerto Chimenez (2010-03):

This little town is nothing but a gateway to the nearby national park: Corcovado.

Corcovado National Park (2010-03):

The park is brilliant, you can do trips of 2 days or more to go from Carate to Sirena along the beach that touches the jungle and back along a different track right through the jungle. 

Hike corcovado NP (2010-03):

A very nice 2 day hike in and out of the jungle.

Tilaran (2017-04):

The small town is not a place to get stuck, but I was lucky to be invited to stay in someones home.

La Fortuna (2017-04):

A tourist hub because of many tourist attractions. The Arenal volcano was in the clouds though and for me it was just a boring place I was happy to leave.

Cahuita (2017-04):

Boasting a nice piece of jungle at the beach, this relaxed place does attract quite a few tourists. It was a nice place to hang out for a day.

Puerto Viejo (2017-04):

Tourists hanging around on and near the beach, a boring place in many ways but popular nevertheless.

Tortuguero (2017-04):

Interesting place, a tourist town in the middle of the jungle at the Atlantic. Getting there was already worth it, and because I saw so many animals already I did not bother getting into the actual national park.

Manuel Antonio NP (2017-04):

Beautiful spot with many local tourists on the beach and quite a few animals in the jungle. I was happy to see sloths and that was not the only thing.
