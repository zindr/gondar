Italy

Rome (2015-02):

A beautiful historic city with its Colosseum, perfect for a city trip when you are not swamped by tourists.

Vatican City (2015-02):

The smallest country in the world, a museum within Rome, a big cathedral, filled with history.

Tre Cime di Lavadero (Sexten) (2015-07):

Maybe the most famous hike in the dolomites can be started from Sexten, going up to Tre Cime from the north taking about 6 hours.

Cortina d`Ampezzo (2015-07):

One of the few slightly bigger cities, quite alright, catering for tourists.

Crado da Lago (2015-07):

A four hour hike not far from Cortina starting from Passo Di Giau to the lake is one of the many options, not too spectacular though but nice.

Venice (2015-07):

It is clear why this city draws so many tourists each year, a very nice place to visit for its peculiar atmosphere with the many canals.

Verona (2015-07):

Often recommended Verona is another city worth paying a small visit.

Vigo di Fassa (2015-07):

Vigo is a tourist hub for walking the nearby mountains, and in winter it is a ski resort. For us it was the end of the hike.

Campitello di Fassa (2015-07):

Campitello is yet another tourist hub to ski and hike. For us it was the start of the hike across the Catanaccio.

Chiavenna (2020-06):

A camping stop in cheaper Italy offered a good opportunity for a short stroll in the small but nice old town, and a pizza nearby our camping.
