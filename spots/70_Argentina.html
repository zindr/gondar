Argentina

Buenos Aires (2011-12):

Refreshing... elegance and refinement in the Americas, you do not find that much around. Diversity in food, plenty of places to hang out, interesting suburbs, ... very nice place.

Iguazu Falls (2011-12):

Hugh waterfalls, second widest in the world, in a nice albeit somewhat gamepark like setting. It was spectacular.

Puerto Iguazu (2011-12):

Gateway to the second widest falls in the world, at the time an expensive city that was well equipped for dealing with the flow of tourists.

Corrientes (2011-12):

In transit... just a walk through.

Resistencia (2011-12):

Hitch-hiking is not easy here... if you are a man... very different for women in this macho culture. A simple transit...

Mercedes (2011-12):

On my way to Ibera national park, this town was not too bad.

Pellegrini (2011-12):

An extremely boring looking small tourist town, in the middle of the Ibera wetlands, way to expensive for what it is...

El Calafate (2011-12):

Tourist hub for the glacier Perito Moreno, expensive and set up for tourists.

Perito Moreno Glacier (2011-12):

The biggest glacier, moving and making a lot of noise... impressive. Some go walk on top of it, expensive and not worth it for most. There are touristy boat trips as well, not recommended.

El Chalten (2012-01):

The base for hiking Fitz Roy and surroundings, the town is simple and expensive, set up for tourists because there is nothing else.

Laguna Torre (2012-01):

For hiking, it is a beautiful spot, and it takes a few days to enjoy.

Fitz Roy Hike (2012-01):

For hiking, it is a beautiful spot, all over.

Piedra Buena (2012-01):

Cities are boring here in Argentina, and some are really boring.

Los Antiguos (2012-01):

Border towns, just passing through.

Mendoza (2012-01):

In transit, just walking through, I do not think I have missed much here.

Cafayate (2012-01):

A tourist hub near the Quebradas, popular with the local youngsters on their holiday.

Angastaco (2012-01):

A very small town on the road in the Quebradas, surrounded by brilliant scenery.

Molinos (2012-01):

A small town on the road in the Quebradas, brilliant scenery around it. 

Cachi (2012-01):

The recommended tourist spot... a tiny little town, it was nice but it takes only 5 minutes to cross it.

Quebrada Tres Cruces - Amphitheatro (2012-01):

A few rock formations... yeah well, it is a beautiful place to travel through.

Coronel Moldes (2012-01):

A quick stopover when hitchhiking back.

La Caldera (2012-01):

In transit... to the north, hitchhiking. The scenery is already nice.

Hike / Hitchike north of Salta (2012-01):

Going north I walked a lot, the scenery was nice.

Purmamarca (2012-01):

Nice rocks in this tiny village, very popular with local youngsters for hanging out.

Salta (2012-01):

The centre city of all the Quebradas, a tourist area. I did not like it that much, it suffers from being boring like so many other places in the Americas.

Maimara (2012-01):

Another little town in the Quebradas, full with local youngsters.

Tilcara (2012-01):

The most touristy of little towns in the Quebradas. A nice and charming place.

Humahuaca (2012-01):

A bit of a hub city in the Quebradas, a bit bigger than the rest.

Iruya (2012-01):

A tiny tourist town up in the Altiplano, the scenery is again great.

Iruya Hike (2012-01):

A hike back from Iruya was really nice, with very nice scenery around.

La Quiaca (2012-01):

Get in, get out... border town
