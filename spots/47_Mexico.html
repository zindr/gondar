Mexico

Mexico City (2009-12):

A very nice centre Mexico City has and it probably is well worth a few days, easy to get in and out with bus or plain.

Taxco (2009-12):

Not far from the capital this city may give a second perspective on Mexican city life

Grutas de Cacahuamilpa (2009-12):

A good kilometre of spectacular cave scenery, unfortunately I shared it with hundreds of school children and the obligatory guide that shows coincidental features like rocks that look like a head or something.

Cuernavaca (2009-12):

A slightly boring but beautiful place, it clearly is an upmarket place.

Cancun (2009-12):

To be honest, this one I simply passed by because it is everything tourists like me do not want.

Valladolid (2009-12):

A nice entrance to the Yucatan peninsula, Valladolid allows for some sidetrips and is on the way to Chitzen Itza.

Chitzen Itza (2009-12):

Maya city of the early days which is especially impressive because of its echoing system. A visit would take about two hours and I think should not be missed when around.

Merida (2010-01):

A Mexican city, not much more... allowing for a few side trips. For me it was the place to celebrate New Years, clearly not a festive highlight in Mexico.

Campeche (2010-01):

Another Mexican city but smaller than Merida and thus somewhat more boring, still it clearly is the real life.

Palenque (2010-01):

Just a smaller city but I preferred it over the tourist hangouts a little bit out of the city in the direction of the nearby Palenque ruins which are ok to visit.

San Cristobal (2010-01):

My favourite city in Mexico, although somewhat touristed. Some sidetrips are possible, do not miss the crazy church in the nearby village San Juan Chamula.

Cañon del Sumidero (2010-01):

I also enjoyed a trip to the Cañon del Sumidero, two hours of boat tripping for spotting wildlife amidst a canon.
