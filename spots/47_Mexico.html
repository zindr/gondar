Mexico

Mexico City (2009-12):

A very nice center Mexico City has and it probably is well worth a few days, easy to get in and out with bus or plain.

Taxco (2009-12):

Not far from the capital this city may give a second perspective on Mexican city life

Grutas de Cacahuamilpa (2009-12):

A good kilometer of spectacular cave scenery, unfortunately I shared it with hundreds of school children and the obligatory guide that shows coincidental features like rocks that look like a head or something.

Cuernavaca (2009-12):

A slightly boring but beautiful place, it clearly is an upmarket place.

Cancun (2009-12):

To be honest, this one I simply passed by because it is everything tourists like me do not want.

Valladolid (2009-12):

A nice entrance to the Yucatan peninsula, Valladolid allows for some side-trips and is on the way to Chitzen Itza.

Chitzen Itza (2009-12):

Maya city of the early days which is especially impressive because of its echoing system. A visit would take about two hours and I think should not be missed when around.

Merida (2010-01):

A Mexican city, not much more... allowing for a few side trips. For me it was the place to celebrate New Years, clearly not a festive highlight in Mexico.

Cenotes Cuzama (2010-01):

A Dysney like side trip to cenotes like Chelentun with horse carriages and swimming in a blue water cave, why not, it is beautiful and we are tourists.

Campeche (2010-01):

Another Mexican city but smaller than Merida and thus somewhat more boring, still it clearly is the real life.

Palenque (2010-01):

Just a smaller city but I preferred it over the tourist hangouts a little bit out of the city in the direction of the nearby Palenque ruins which are OK to visit.

Yaxchilan (2010-01):

As part of a tour and a bit far out the temples of Yaxchilan are quite nice, although the concert of howler monkeys may have added to the experience, and it is the jungle alright.

Bonampak (2010-01):

On route to Yaxchilan you would visit Bonampak with its small ruins with colored designs.

San Cristobal (2010-01):

My favorite city in Mexico, although somewhat touristed. Some side-trips are possible, do not miss the crazy church in the nearby village San Juan Chamula.

Sumidero Canyon (2010-01):

I also enjoyed a trip to the Sumidero Canyon, two hours of boat tripping for spotting wildlife amidst a canon.

Teotihuacan (2023-04):

I guess this long lasting proof of old civilizations is worth the small trip from the city.

Chihuahua (2023-04):

City up north, nice for a walk around, skipped the dunes though.

Creel (2023-04):

Small touristy town at the verge of the copper canyon, you can take the train here.

El Divisadero (2023-04):

Tiny overlook over the copper canyon, possible to reach by bus from Creel.

El Fuerte (2023-04):

Why go all the way by train, you can take the bus from here, this small but nice enough city.

Los Mochis (2023-04):

The end of the line for the copper canyon trail, just a city.

Topolobampo (2023-04):

Want to hit the beach, get here from Los Mochis and relax.

Tijuana (2023-04):

Border city, with all that comes with it, interesting enough for a day.

Tijuana Beach (2023-05):

Want to hit the beach, get here from Tijuna and have a drink.
