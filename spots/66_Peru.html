Peru

Lima (2011-11):

The capital had a bad reputation but things improved a lot I think. I enjoyed my short stay hanging around in the historical center the first time, and also Miraflores and Baranco the second time.

Ayacucho (2011-11):

Growing in tourism, this city is a nice introduction to Peru. Maybe in future the nearby jungle will open up, after the insurgencies stopped a decade ago.

Andahuaylas (2011-11):

first strike, no transport... so, you get stuck in places.

Abancay (2011-11):

in transit, trying to get more south, here it can take time, luckily the scenery sometimes gets amazing.

Cuzco (2011-11):

The gateway to the Machu Pichu, this city bring all amenities. It is quite nice here.

Agua Calientes (2011-11):

Final stop before Machu Pichu, a tourist hub that you may not want to stay longer than necessary.

Machu Pichu (2011-11):

World famous, a beautiful but over-prized set of ruins in a very beautiful setting.

Santa Teresa (2011-11):

Transit town to Machu Pichu with local transport, if you want to avoid to ridiculously over-prized train.

Santa Maria (2011-11):

Transit to Machu Pichu on local transport, it takes some few hours walking but this saves you many many Euros.

Puerto Maldonado (2011-11):

An entry point into the jungle, starting up since improvement of roads towards it. It provides easy access to the jungle, I regret that I did not take the opportunity.

Juliaca (2011-11):

The regions commercial center, and not really a place to visit too long.

Puno (2011-11):

The entry point for visiting Lake Titicaca and its over commercialized floating villages. I would not bother.

Arequipa (2011-11):

Colonial buildings and tourists... many like it, I think it is a bit boring.

Chivay (2011-11):

Transit on the way to the Colca Canyon, just a town.

Cabanaconde (2011-11):

Entry to hiking the Colca Canyon, a small town.

Tapay (2011-11):

Tiny little town in the mountains of the Colca Canyon, a place where I put up my tent. I loved it.

Tacna (2011-11):

Transit, on route to Chile.

San Ignacio border (2019-06):

Tiny border town with beautiful scenery at the Ecuadorian side.

Chachapoyas (2019-06):

Nice town, perfect for visiting nearby sites and hikes.

Cohechan (2019-06):

Tiny town, possible start of the Gran Vilaya trek and south of historical statues.

Huaylla Belen (2019-06):

Beautiful valley, start or first stop in the Gran Vilaya trek to Congon and over the Yumal pass.

Kuelap (2019-06):

Historical ruins, near the end of the Gran Vilaya trek, reachable by collectivo from Choctamal after the Yumal pass.

Gocta Waterfall (2019-06):

Brilliant cloud forest on route to the 3rd or 16th highest drop in the world, depending on how it is measured.

Cajamarca (2019-06):

Especially nice scenery coming from Chachapoyas, but otherwise ok city to hang out for a day.

Trujillo (Chan Chan - Huaca del Sol) (2019-06):

Beautiful Plaza de Armas, and offering side trips to nearby (5 km) Chan Chan ruins (west) and Huaca de la Luna/Sol (south/east).

Huaraz (2019-06):

The trekkers mecca, flanked by the Cordillera Blanca, Huascaran and not for from Huayhuash, fully equiped for tourist, even with vegan food and outdoor shops.

Laguna 69 (2019-06):

Beautiful hike up to an OK lake, good to acclimatize with a more serious trek in mind.

Llamac (2019-07):

Possible start/end of the Huayhuash circuit.

3 lake view (2019-07):

Famous viewpoint, first highlight of the circuit.

Gunog Hot Spring (2019-07):

Relaxing bathing in beautiful scenery, possibly with a beer.

San Antonio pass (2019-07):

Famous viewpoint, second highlight of the circuit.

Huayllapa (2019-07):

First and last town on the circuit, with a few shops.

2 lake view (2019-07):

Famous viewpoint, third highlight of the circuit.

Paracas (2019-07):

Touristy town, good for hikes in coastal dunes of the nearby national park (10 km south/west) and a boat trip to the poor mans Galapagos (Islas Balestas).

Huacacina (Ica) (2019-07):

Dunes around a lake, in miniature tourist village nearby Ica.
