Bolivia

La Paz (2011-11):

De facto the capital of Bolivia, high up in the mountains. It has a few nice parts, and the vibe is nice too, good for a few days. You might want to tackle the 6000m. high mountain that is near.

Oruro (2011-11):

Just a Bolivian city, nothing too special, it was our entry into the country and already our first confrontation with a road block.

Uyuni (2011-11):

The gateway to the Salar, it is a small city which knows how to deal with tourists. There is not so much reason to linger on.

Salar (2011-11):

The biggest salt plains in the world, amazing, a highlight in Bolivia including the cacti island in the middle somewhere.

Alti Plano 1 (2011-11):

A three day tour brings you from the Salar to this place and back, with its geysers.

Alti Plano 2 (2011-11):

Through the Altiplano with lakes at high altitude, of different colours with different fauna.

Tupiza (2011-11):

Quebradas, just some beautiful mountains to walk in... near this town. The town is nothing of real interest, but ok.

Potosi (2011-11):

The highest city in the world, it seems... on transit. You could go and visit the mines, but beware that it may be unhealthy doing so.

La Cumbre (2011-11):

The start of the Choro trek, a popular and interesting hike down through different layers of vegetation. We loved it, it took us 1.5 days to reach Chairo.

Challapampa (2011-11):

Camping the night halfway the Choro trek.

Chairo (2011-11):

The end of the Choro trek, and luckily we could take a truck out in the morning going to La Paz.

Hike Choro trek (2011-11):

A very nice downhill trek of 2 days.

Rurrenabaque (2011-12):

The town for trips to the jungle or the pampas, we did the latter. The town was simple, ok but nothing special.

Santa Maria (2011-12):

Gateway to the Pampas, a small place on route.

the Pampas (2011-12):

Diverse wildlife in these wetlands, fishing for piranhas and swimming with dolphins, caipibaras, caiman...

Trinidad (2011-12):

Nice city to just hang out... it reminded me of Africa, a bit rough but interesting.

Santa Cruz (2011-12):

Boring modern town, with hummers on the street... I do not get why it is often recommended.

Samaipata (2011-12):

A tourist place near Amboro national park, good for visiting the cloud forest. We did not, we should have, because the town itself is nothing of interest.

Villamontes (2011-12):

Real Bolivian city, a bit boring though, but also a bit interesting... the gateway to Paraguay, although you could just as well take the bus from Santa Cruz that also passes here.

Sucre (2011-12):

The political capital of Bolivia is a nice colonial town, a bit too nice and tidy for me.

Tarija (2012-01):

Just a city with some possibilities for tourists, with wine making tours for example. I was stuck there because of another ridiculous set of road blocks.

Yacuiba (2011-12):

Just another border town to pass, we got there but turned back.

Villazon (2012-01):

The main border town with Argentina.
