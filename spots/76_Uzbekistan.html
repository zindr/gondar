Uzbekistan

Tashkent (2012-09):

Undoubtedly the most developed city in central Asia, you will even find bars to go out. I enjoyed it, but it is still hard recommending it.

Samarkand (2012-09):

UNESCO, ahem. A place with a name like few others, like Timbuktu. Now, it is a tourist hub with a few very nice 14-15 century building good to keep you busy for a day.

Bukhara (2012-09):

UNESCO, ahem. More of a city than Khiva and more diverse than the tourist centre in Samarkand, this place is my favourite of the three.

Khiva (2012-09):

UNESCO, well yeah... it is kinda cool to walk around a mud walled city with a history of being on the silk road and the main spot for merchandising slaves.

Urgench (2012-09):

The main city nearby Khiva, as an entry and exit point... use it only for that. Even the market here is not a place to enjoy, and the rest is just ordinary mainstream old sovjet style

Nukus (2012-08):

A Russian type of city with a recommended art museum (no idea why)... and of course, an entry/exit point from Turkmenistan.

Kokand (2012-09):

Here to cross the border and to find out that the road to the border was closed... so, sleep and go back again... I stayed with my taxi driver in a nearby village, weird and interesting.
