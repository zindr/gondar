Pakistan

Islamabad (2002-10):

The squared administrative capital was a quite interesting days out. But in terms of tourist attractions, there is nothing.

Rawalpindi (2002-10):

The dirty, polluted and overcrowded twin-city of Islamabad where the people actually live is also quite interesting. The slums are dramatic.

Lahore (2002-10):

The most tourist city, at the border with India. It has some attractions, nice market streets and a beautiful fort. Do not miss the Sufi dance performances in nearby temples. 

Quetta (2002-09):

Bordering Afghanistan, this city is a mix of different people, street and markets, interesting place although there is not much for tourists.

Peshawar (2002-10):

The gateway to Afghanistan through the beautiful Kyberpass, this city is OK for spending a day with an interesting mix of people and a vivid market place to explore.

Gilgit (2002-10):

I left quickly, there is not much to do but the scenery around is very nice.

Minapin (2002-10):

The tiny town had one guesthouse that is surprisingly good. The only reason to be here is the hike up to base-camp of the Rakaposhi mountain, and that is brilliant. It is a four to five hour hike up there, and you could sleep at basecamp.

Karimabad (2002-10):

It is a beautiful valley, and the town itself is nice too... and you could do an OK day hike up and down to base-camp of Ultar mountain.
