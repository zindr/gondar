Greece (1995)

greece

[ 21 days ]

Finally, I would make it to Greece, the Peloponnese, hungry for taking in as much as possible, even the most boring museum, all was good at the time. And there were some very nice things to do as well, partly with my girlfriend, and some days we met up with friends that were also traveling there at the time but they were mostly into beaching.

Turkey (1996)

turkey

[ 28 days ]

Traveling started to be a bit more adventurous, an occasional hitchhike, the Turkish culture that was very different from ours, especially venturing into the east of the country. Standing on Mount Nimrod, looking east at the Euphrates, I knew... I was going to go in that direction, one day.

Indonesia (1998)

indonesia

[ 28 days ]

First time, really out there, admittedly, it was not always easy to digest, traveling in a country that was suffering economically, with serious poverty. But the traveling was very rewarding, adventurous, with several attractions and many new experiences. The speed of traveling was now much higher, it felt as if we could take a bus to the next destination at any time, and it felt as if we did.

, We crossed Java to Bali and back. Especially the first was fun, for its people, and interesting for its contrast with back home. Bali was much more upmarket and a clear showcase of the impact mass tourism can have.

Middle East (2004)

egypt - jordan - syria

[ 23 days ]

With the start of my PhD the next month, I had a bit of time to further my link from Indonesia to Turkey into Egypt.

Egypt has a lot to offer, I was especially blown away by the temples of Karnak, and also Jordan with its desert and Petra were a real treat although I did not like how they treated tourists. Syria, that was just fun to be, such friendly and hospitable people, still, with so few tourists around.

Balkan, eastern side (2008)

hungary - serbia - bosnia herzegovina - bulgaria - macedonia - kosovo

[ 17 days ]

With only a few weeks left before I started my post-doc, I left for the Balkan two days after I defended my Phd, with a bus at such short notice, to Budapest.

While not boasting very many things, I had enough nice and interesting experiences to make it worthwhile.

Budapest was great, and in Serbia I enjoyed the hospitality of the tourist information offices that appeared all to happy with my walking in. Bulgaria was mainly cocktail country, and aside from that I had a taste from Kosovo that you had to enter from Macedonia because of the issues with Serbia. Sarajevo was easier to just take in with a side trip.

Madagascar (2013)

madagascar

[ 29 days ]

If ever I was going to do a honeymoon... I considered it as expensive enough for that. Interesting African country, typical, just spending 50% of the time waiting for transport out, to wherever.

Just being there for me was kinda a highlight, good vibe, nice people around, fun to talk to, and I had some good times with my couchsurf, one of my favorite countries in Africa for sure.

China (2014)

china

[ 31 days ]

If it were up to me, I would have combined China with Mongolia or Kyrgyzstan, but thanks to the annoying visa rules in China, I just traveled there, but I did go for it, the full 12000 kilometers, all in one month, east, west and south. It is a big country, so obviously there are some interesting sites, loved Bejing that coincidentally was fairly smog-free that week in preparation of the Asia summit, I am so often so lucky when I am traveling, you would not believe.

Balkan, western side (2014)

croatia - bosnia herzegovina - montenegro - albania - macedonia - greece

[ 27 days ]

Having crossed through the eastern side of the Balkan, now I had a bit of time to cross the western side, hitchhiking the first part up until Podgorica where I would meet my girlfriend and move on. For me it was also a trip that I could combine with the Meteora in Greece, left out the first time I was in Greece.

It was not easy the first few weeks hitchhiking, but I learned that that was just great, sleeping in a squat, on the street, in a mosque, to give an example. In Croatia it went slowly, also in BIH but there I ended up having great experiences, and lucky me, I got a ride through all of Montenegro. From Albania onward, with my girlfriend around, travel was at a more leisurely pass, all the way to Greece. The hike in Macedonia was a great choice.

Europe, up north (2014)

denmark - norway

[ 20 days ]

Traveling by car, not something I have done much, but to Norway it seemed the best way, it was. Although it is a long way up, once there, Norway is simply brilliant, but very very expensive.

Norway is all about barren landscapes, fjord, a few beautiful hikes, and just driving. Denmark was just on the way, well, to say it like that would not fully do it justice.

West Africa, south side (2015)

ghana - togo - benin

[ 17 days ]

With only a few weeks to spare before my next contract, a short trip in West Africa would be ideal, especially because of the cheap airline ticket, visiting Togo and Benin. There is not so much to do but the people and the vibe are very nice.

The highlight probably was in the north going from one country to the other, hiking to UNESCO area for a full day to end up in a local hut for the night, you can not get more real than that.

Europe, up north (2015)

sweden - norway

[ 18 days ]

Still very expensive, but the first experience of Norway made us want more, we even almost took a flight the first time to have some extra days in the north north, but we did not, we just came back for it, combining it with the Sweden.

The Lofoten and around did offer a very nice road trip, beautiful scenery and weather, with a few fun hikes, and just driving. Stockholm was very nice indeed.

Southern Africa, again (2016)

south africa - lesotho - eswatini

[ 22 days ]

Filling in gaps, this brought me back to South Africa that I only spent 5 days before. So with Drakensberg in mind, for hiking, it would be a perfect road trip that included Lesotho and Swaziland, now eSwatini.

The nicest about this trip was the diversity, a first part in the superb mountains, a second visiting game parks, and a last to dive into civilization. Lesotho was just adventurous driving through the highlands, much more fun and interesting than Swaziland, but ok, that was alright too. I was positively surprised by South Africa as well, and we made a perfect choice to drop our car for the final few days to explore Jo'burg and Pretoria.

Central Asia, again (2017)

kazakhstan - kyrgyzstan - tajikistan - belarus

[ 26 days ]

The enthusiasm about Tajikistan that I showed my girlfriend after I saw her last time, in Bishkek, in 2012, must have been impressive, she was going to go with me this time, to Tajikistan, now our all time favorite. With Belarus opening up a bit, giving an effortless 5 day fly-in-fly-out visa, that was an opportunity not to be missed too.

The hiking was brilliant, in the Fann mountains, what an amazing 4 days, and also the hike in Kyrgyzstan that we could not do last time, great few days, of course, in this part of the world just moving around, through the Pamir and in the Wakhan, it all is just great. Kazakhstan, that was just a stopover, not as nice as the previous time I was there. Belarus on the other hand was interesting enough, lucky to have been there at 950 years Minsk.

West Africa, west point (2018)

senegal - gambia

[ 16 days ]

If you see a very cheap ticket to a country that you passed through too quickly last time, book it. Last time, a bit afraid of the costs of visiting Dakar, I skipped it, but glad to have returned. And when around, I also took in small Gambia.

It was maybe not the type of highlight traveling, but I like Africa for what it is, a place to just be. I had some nice contacts there as well, loved the Casamance scenery.

South East Asia, covid-19 (2020)

thailand - malaysia

[ 18 days ]

I was going to visit southern Thailand, and a national park in Malaysia before going further down and up to Tasmania and Japan, and then, COVID-19 came. So I was stuck in Kuala Lumpur for about 10 days, luckily after the national park visit.

I missed the flight to Australia by a day, but maybe that is a good thing, it would have been a more difficult place to get out of during the pandemic, presumably, all in all it turned out ok for me.
