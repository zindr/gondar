city trips (2001)

paris - amsterdam - prague - krakov - lisbon - madrid - barcelona - london - berlin - rome

[ 3-5 days ]

The occasional city trip, especially when you know someone there or if you happen to pass by because of work, can be just the perfect few days to get out of it from time to time.

Paris I knew people, a great way to explore a city, Amsterdam we had a free apartment, Prague, just because it was highly recommended, like Krakow. Lisbon, London, Madrid and Barcelona, thanks to my job, Berlin and Rome, because these are the ones left out.

mini trips (2013)

poland - luxembourg - spain - portugal - wales & england - scotland

[ 4+4+5+5+6+6 days ]

Mini vacations, they can be fun if you only have little time. In Poland my car got stolen, so my trip was cut short, Luxembourg is next door, good for a few days, in Portugal the north was explored and in Spain the south, lucky to be a midst festivities in Seville and Porto.

France (1993)

france - monaco

[ 14 days ]

First trip, independent that is. My girlfriend and I went to the south of France because she was not allowed to join me to Greece. No idea what we were going to do, so we did not do anything really.

Naive we were, I just did not know anything, but in a first trip, being away with the girlfriend, it does not matter much, does it. So we live on the cheapest food around, Mac Donalds and Quick, and took the train up and down places without paying, shame on us.

Europe, eastern side (2010)

ukraine - moldova - slovakia - austria - poland - czech republic

[ 15 days ]

With only a few weeks before my next contract as a post-doc, I went for more nearby east of Europe, nearby but not really.

I did love Ukraine, especially the train rides, so close and yet so different, Moldova, very interesting because it was so boring, very weird. The other countries, well, they were the side-trips at the time, Vienna in Austria, Olomouc in Czech, and Slovakia because it was in the middle.

Black Sea, north-west (2012)

romania - ukraine

[ 15 days ]

Romania I could combine with the Crimea in Ukraine that I left out 2 years before, so Romania was more because I had not been there than anything else.

While the Ukraine would be my taste, Romania was ok. The Crimea did feel a bit different, with a huge Russian influence, I guess that is now even more so. I should have spent some time hiking in Romania, but I was not prepared sufficiently, so I missed several opportunities.

Black Sea, south-east (2012)

turkey - georgia

[ 15 days ]

Georgia would be the nicest of the three Caucasus countries, no time to explore them all. But very nice it was, a real treat. I did not have more time, when you are given unemployment benefits, you can not just leave the country on long trips.

Especially for the interaction with the lovely people over there that country made a difference, the very tasteful bread, some nice hikes too.

Iceland road trip (2015)

iceland

[ 8 days ]

We made the tour around Iceland, beautiful place, a lot of kilometers in the country that did not go dark. While we did some hikes, it ended up being foremost a real road trip.

The country felt a bit like a huge vacation theme park, you do not go there for its cities and towns, but the scenery was superb. So, we did see the geyser, and various places seemed otherworldly enough to make it worth it.

Italy road trip (2015)

italy - austria - liechtenstein - germany

[ 12 days ]

Having seen a picture of the Dolomites made it clear, I wanted to go, so we did with a road trip all the way to Italy. While there were definetly also some spots on the way and back, Italy was our main goal.

The hikes were straightforward and very nice, but quickly it turned more into a road trip in which we enjoyed our coffee in some tiny old town, or a pizza.

Kazakhstan (2016)

kazakhstan

[ 8 days ]

Still with a few free days on my job, heard that the country recently opened up by offering visa free stays up to 15 days, together with the inflation and a cheap airline ticket, this is the time to hop over. While clearly not a tourist hub, it was quite interesting, not at all easy, especially without knowledge of Russian language, moving around can be tough.

Balkan, central (2018)

macedonia - kosovo - albania - montenegro - serbia - hungary

[ 13 days ]

Rain kept us from hiking in the north of Albania last time, so now that was our main goal, to hike from kosovo all the way through to Albania along the Montenegro border, through the accursed mountains. The 4 day hike was really nice, and then it was simply moving upwards to Budapest.

It is interesting to end up in the same place a second time, surprising how perspectives can be different, like for me to visit Podgorica, or Serbia that we now did with a car rental instead of buses.

Ireland (2019)

ireland

[ 9 days ]

Another small trip, with especially Dublin and the cliffs of Moher in mind, without too many other expectations.

It was another road trip, with especially the atmosphere to keep us going, amazing how people enjoy themselves with live local music in bars.

Morocco hiking trip (2019)

morocco

[ 13 days ]

I did not hike the Atlas, last time that I was in Morocco, I should have. But luckily, with a few weeks to spare, Morocco is nearby enough for a quick second run.

I did a small hike in the rift valley, but the absolute highlight, even much more than hoped for was the M'Goun, probably because I was so unprepared and I went fully basic, it all worked out.

Switzerland hiking (2020)

switzerland

[ 12 days ]

Although I have spent much time in Switzerland as a kid and teenager, with family and youth holidays, I felt that I did not yet visit it truly. Of course, the budget does limit options in this country, severely.

Maybe I expected a bit too much from it, but it was nothing more than OK, with nice hikes but often overrun by tourists, local because of covid, brought up the mountain with cable cars, not my thing. The pass from Italy was maybe most memorable.

Iceland hiking trip (2020)

iceland

[ 13 days ]

I knew that one day I was going to go back to Iceland to do the hike that we could not do last time because it was too early in the year: the Landmannalaugar hike. With enough time so that we could add one other hike and be flexible enough to deal with unlucky weather, we just had to decide there what else to do.

The main hike was the absolute success, superb views in weather that was as good as it gets. So, after 4 days we kinda did what we came for, so we had fun relaxing and adding in a few smaller hikes.
