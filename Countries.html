Belgium

I like my home country Belgium where I have been living all of my life. I stayed in the Flemish student city Leuven after my studies, near Brussels. For tourists I would probably recommend the historic city of Ghent rather than the open air museum Bruges, and Brussels where I work and live from time to time has its beauty too. Try Duvel and La Chouffe.

Places
Brussels - Leuven - Antwerp - Ghent - Kortrijk - Hasselt - Liege - High Fens

Germany

Maybe not the country to love spontaneously, as a Belgium, but they are probably very similar. Berlin is just great, I could even go and live there, for the rest I have not spent much time there and it was ok.

Places
Berlin - Eifel - Monschau - Gemund - Eushkirchen - Oberstdorf - Heidelberg

Switzerland

A beautiful country suited particularly well for families and old people, or youth camps, this is also how I got to know this very expensive country. The mountains are great for hiking but they are, like the country, a bit too polished.

Places
Disentis - Sankt Moritz - Joriseen - Casaccia hike - Tamboalp hike - Göscheneralp hike - Five Lakes trail - Grindelwald - Bern

Austria

The mountains provide a less spectacular and less costly alternative to the Swiss Alps. Some of the cities are quite nice, and Vienna is impressive.

Places
Vienna - Innsbruck - Salzburg - Umhausen

Netherlands

Our closest neighbour, we even share the language. I used to go there a lot for buying weed in their famous coffee shops, until I quit smoking.

Places
Amsterdam - Maastricht - Sittard - Eindhoven - Tilburg - Arnhem - Enschede

France

The world nr 1 tourist country in terms of visitor numbers. I went to the south for my first trip as a 19 year old with my girlfriend (AUG, 1993; 14 days; 25€/day). I went back several times for visiting friends. The country has a lot to offer but do not count on too much friendliness if on the tourist track.

Places
Paris - Versailles - Mont Saint Michel - Saint Brieuc - Nice - Cannes - Nimes - Castillon-du-Gard - Mulhouse - Colmar

Luxembourg

Known to us because it had cheap cigarettes and petrol, this tiny country offers a few short hikes.

Places
Luxembourg - Echternach - Consdorf

United Kingdom

The other side of the canal and yet far away, not geographically but culturally. Diverse and cosmopolitan London (3+4 days) filled with ex-pats is clearly an exception. Scotland boasts some really nice scenery, especially in the far north and Skye (MAY 2016; 6 days) but also Wales and the Lake District have nice hikes on offer (JUNE 2017; 7 days).

Places
London - Canterbury - Cambridge - Edinburgh - Hike Meall a Bhuachaille - Kinlochewe - Plockton - Portree - Hike Old Man of Storr - Hike Quiraing - Hike Fairy Pools Glen Brittle - Hike Bruach Na Frithe - Fort William - Hike Steall Falls - Hike Cobbler - Killin - Llandudno - Caernarfon - Nantlle Ridge Hike - Betws-y-Coed Town and Hike - Pontcysyllte Aqueduct - Chester - Liverpool - Scaffel Pike Hike - Ravenglass - Buttermere - Windermere - Clitheroe - Manchester Airport

Monaco

Is it a country ? This city state is easily visited as a day trip when you are near (AUG 1993; 1 day), well, a half day trip would do.

Places
Monaco

Greece

The cheap nearby country to visit when I was young, so I did a long time ago when I visited mainly the Peloponessos (SEP, 1995; 21 days; budget 20€/day) with its historical sites and OK beaches. During another trip I ended up in the north (JUN, 2014; 4 days; budget 30€/day).

Places
Athens - Delfi - Itea - Naflion - Tolo - Epidavros Theatre - Mykene - Mavromati - Methoni - Sparta - Olimpia - Ioannina - Kalambaka - Thessaloniki

Turkey

Europe and Asia, with marvelous Istanbul in between. I first visited the western and central part (SEP, 1995; 28 days; budget=15€/day) with its historical sites, OK beaches and beautiful nature like amazing Cappadocia. The second time I just crossed it to the east on my way to Iran (SEP, 2002; 7 days; budget=10€/day) with vast natural scenery and untouristed cities. On my way to Georgia I passed by the northern part as well (MAY, 2012; 2 days). Turkey has a lot to offer for a diverse type of tourists, I enjoyed the vastness and scenery of the east most.

Places
Istanbul - Canakkale - Pergamon - Selcuk - Ephesus - Guzelcamli - Karakasu (Aphrodisias) - Pamukkale - Antalya - Aspendos Theatre - Thermessos - Olympos - Manavgat - Nevsehir - Goreme - Ilhara Gorge - Underground Cities - Uchisar - Avanos - Malatya - Nemrut Dagi - Urfa - Harran - Dyarbakir - Mardin - Dogubayazit - Trabzon

Indonesia

This big country has a lot to offer, it is huge and diverse in both culture and nature, with volcanoes, wildlife, jungle, beaches, historical sites. I first visit interesting Java and over-touristed Bali (SEP, 1998; 28 days; 3.5&euro/day), later mountainous Sumatra (SEP, 2001; 14 days; 5€/day) and then the east (APR, 2013; 30 days; 9&euro/day) covering the islands of beautiful Sulawesi, almost tourist free Flores and Western Timor.

Places
Jakarta - Yogyakarta - Borobudur - Karangpandan - Candi Sukuk - Probolinggo - Mount Bromo - Banyuwangi - Kawah Ijen - Gilimanuk - Denpasar - Ubud - Kediri - Tanah Lot - Klungkung - Padangbai - Bratan Lake - Lovina Beach - Alas Purwo NP - Dieng Plateau - Sumenep - Pamekasan - Pangandaran - Bogor - Bukittinggi - Lake Maninjau - Parapat - Lake Toba - Berestagi - Ketambe (Leusser NP) - Medan - Dumai - Makassar - Rammang Rammang - Rantepao - Palu - Gimpu (Lore Lindu) - Walk Sulawesi Rantepao 3 - Hike Sulawesi Rantepao 1 - Walk Sulawesi Rantepao 2 - Maumere - Marapokot - Mbay - Bajawa - Flores villages Hike - Watijuli - Tolulelo - Mala - Kelimutu Crater Lake - Kelimutu walk - Moni - Larantuka - Kupang - Kefamenanu - Oilasi - Boti - Soe - Hike Timor Boti

Czech Republic

Beautiful Prague was a city trip (SEP, 2001; 3 days) and I crossed the eastern part as part of another trip (JUN, 2010; 3 days; budget=15€/days). It was nice being there, although nothing was of particular interest.

Places
Prague - Kutna Hora - Olomouc

Malaysia

My first visit was a mere transit (SEP, 2001; 5 days; budget=10€/day), it felt a bit up-market, western like and clean but laid-back and relaxed. In a second trip I enjoyed nice Kuala Lumpur and the different Bornean side at Sarawak (MAY, 2013; 8 days; budget=15€/day). The third time, Covid-19 stopped me here on my way through to Australia (MAR, 2020; 13 days).

Places
Kuala Lumpur - Melaka - Georgetown - Penang - local longhouse - Niah Caves National Park - gas station - Sibu - Kuching - Baku National Park - Semenggoh Wildlife centre - Kampung Benuk - Kota Bharu - Jerantut - Taman Negara NP

Thailand

Overrun by tourists, for obvious reasons, cheap, easy, female friendly, superb food and offering a wide range of possibilities: beaches, jungle, cities, temples, ruins... and for being in the center of South East Asia. This country almost has it all if only it would be a bit more of an adventure. So, I personally would skip it for most, based on what I know from my visit (OCT, 2001; 14 days; budget=6€/day). A short second visit on transit in the south I enjoyed again (MAR, 2020; 5 days; budget=9€/day).

Places
Bangkok - Sukothai - Chiang Mai - Mae Hong Son - Chiang Rai - Chiang Saen - Golden Triangle - Phuket - Hat Yai - Songkhla

Laos

Beautiful country, superb for relaxing with a flavor of adventure as it boasts not only diverse and beautiful scenery but also cheap family run guesthouses, fairly unspoiled minority people, readily available marijuana and opium and lots of boat travel. For me it was maybe just a bit too relaxed with not so many things to do (NOV, 2001; 21 days; budget=6&euro/day).

Places
Vientianne - Luang Prabang - Vang Vieng - Mong Noi - Luang Nam Tha - Muang Sing - Huay Xai - Bolaven - Si Pan Don - Ban Nape

Vietnam

My kinda country (NOV-DEC, 2001; 25 days; budget=8€/day). It was not always easy, with communist-like control over the tourist industry, constant lies to get some of your money but also sweet kind interactive and inquisitive people. Enjoy the natural beauty and get a glimpse of old and war history. Love it, or hate it... oh yeah, always stay calm with a smile, that will pay off big time.

Places
Hanoi - Sapa - Cat Ba Islands - Downhill Hike - Hue - Dalat - Hoi An - Nha Trang - Saigon - Mekong Delta - Cau Dai

Cambodia

The coolest place to be according to lonely planet (DEC, 2001; 10 days; budget=8€/day), with tourism starting up gradually in the post Khmer Rouge era. The temples of Angkor Wat are simply amazing. It was the right place at the right time, I loved it. At the time, large parts of the country in the east were still off limits.

Places
Phnom Penh - Sihanoukville - Siem Reap - Angkor - Battambang

Iran

The first time I crossed this country I did not like it too much (2002, SEP; 16 days; budget=6€/day), interesting it was for its specific culture, historical sites, beautiful mosques, mausoleums and museums, but a bit grey and depressing too. The hospitality towards foreigners is amazing. My second visit was more relaxed and enjoyable (AUG, 2012; 4 days).

Places
Tehran - Tabriz - Yazd - Esfahan - Shiraz - Persepolis - Bam - Kerman - Sirch - Zahedan - Taftan - Kashan - Mashhad - Qouchan

Pakistan

Really thrilling to cross this country (SEP-OCT, 2002; 14 days; budget=6€/day), off-limits at the time according to the embassies because of fundamentalism and the recent 9/11 attack. I skipped dodgy Karachi but otherwise I did not see anything to be afraid off. The country is interesting and beautiful to explore, with its roughness and diversity, and great hospitality. The mountains in the north are superb and easy to hike in.

Places
Islamabad - Rawalpindi - Lahore - Quetta - Peshawar - Gilgit - Minapin - Karimabad

India

Big and diverse, and it was said that you love it or hate it. At my first visit, in the northern part, I was a bit disappointed because it was not that special and at times simply annoying (OCT and NOV, 2002; 30 days; budget=5€/day). My second visit, in the southern part, was an eye opener with much nicer people and despite boasting less tourist attractions (MAR, 2014; 21 days; budget=7€/day).

Places
Delhi - Amritsar - Jodhpur - Pushkar - Jaisailmer - 3 day camel ride - Agra (Taj Mahal) - Varanasi - Darjeeling - 3 day hike - Kolkata - Puri - Kochi - Ernakulam - Alleppey - Kollam - Munnar - Ooty - Sultan Bathery - Mysore - Hampi - Bangalore - Kodaikanal - Trichy - Thanjavur - Madurai - Varkalla

Nepal

Known for its easy access to high mountains, this is a nice country. I have not done any serious hikes during my visit (OCT, 2002; 10 days; budget=5€/day) which I regret, so I have to go back some time. I did enjoy the capital, a bit of trekking in the east and the jungle. It is a relaxed place after visiting some Indian cities in the north.

Places
Kathmandu Valley - Kathmandu - Bhaktapur - Patan - Pokhara - Chitwan NP - Sanauli

Bangladesh

If you want to go where tourists do not, go here, I did (NOV, 2002; 7 days; budget=4€/day). Traveling in this country without guidebook or other information proved to be very difficult. You will be a curiosity and you will have no privacy, it is dirty and overcrowded in the capital but also beautifully green in rural areas, with friendly and helpful people.

Places
Dhaka - Chittagong - Rangamati - Mongla

Myanmar

At the time of my visit (DEC, 2002; 28 days; budget=5€/day) the budget tourist industry was still limited and very rewarding because of its enchanting little villages, the friendliest of people and the amazing nature around. Some places started to be touristed though. Traveling was slow and many places were off limits.

Places
Yangon - Bagan - Pakkoku - Hsipaw - Nyaung Shwe (Inle Lake) - Taunggi - Mandalay - Bago - Thaton - Hpa An - Pyin-Oo-Lwin

Poland

Easy and cheap for a quick visit. The first time my car got stolen (AUG, 2004, 4 days), a second time I was in transit (JUN, 2010, 3 days) and finally I did a quick visit (AUG, 2013, 5 days). It is one of those countries that includes both west and east, interesting.

Places
Warsaw - Wroclaw - Krakow - Auswitsch - Wieliczka - Zakopane (High Tatras)

Egypt

Step back 5 millenniums in Egypt, with the pyramids, temples and tombs, brilliant. The tourist track is deep and annoying, unfortunately, but some sites are worth it nevertheless. I visited the country a first time (SEP, 2004; 8 days and DEC, 2005; 5 days; budget=10€/day). Once of the tourist track people are lovely.

Places
Cairo (Giseh pyramids) - Luxor - Dahab - Aswan - Abu Simbel

Jordan

A few but very very interesting places to visit (SEP, 2004; 7 days; budget=8€/day), beware you will be treated poorly throughout the deep tourist track. Jordanians that are not part of that tourist industry are very nice though, if you meet them.

Places
Amman - Dead Sea - Aqaba - Wadi Rum - Petra - Al Karak - Jerash

Syria

Part of the axis of evil, dixit Bush, hosts very friendly, helpful and hospitable people, I loved it (SEP, 2004; 9 days; budget=6€/day). It did have a few interesting museums and historical sites but the war messed up everything.

Places
Damascus - Homs - Crac de Chevaliers - Hamah - Palmyra - Deir Es Zur - Aleppo - Serjilla (Dead Cities)

Sudan

Rough, and a bit depressing and grey, but it boasts Muslim hospitality and endless desert. I passed it on my way south (DEC, 2005; 7 days; budget=10€/day). Most places were off limits, and the visa was expensive. At the time, the country was not split up yet between the north and (off limits) south Sudan.

Places
Khartoum - Omdurman - Gedaref - Wadi Halfa

Ethiopia

I loved it, its roughness and liveliness (JAN, 2006; 21 days; budget=8€/day). There is a castle even, and churches, temples, mountains, and tribes, and more. Travel is very slow. The food was very monotone and kinda awful and you will spent most of your time waiting. Also tourist attractions tend to be a bit pricey.

Places
Addis Ababa - Gondar - Debre Birhan Selassie - Gondar Castle - Metema - Debark - Simien mountains Hike - Lalibela - Dese - Arba Minch - Khonso - Jinka - Yabelo

Kenya

It was a nice surprise that years of tourism did not affect my visit all that much (JAN, 2006; 13 days; buget=10€/day). I did my first safari here. Part of the north of the country was temporarily off limits, but Nairobi started to be safer again.

Places
Nairobi - Naivasha - Masai Mara - Nakuru - Isiolo - Malaba - Garissa - Wajir - Moyale

Uganda

Beautiful scenery with lots of opportunities like safaris, hikes, rafting... and this is certainly not all. Two weeks still did not seem to be sufficient (FEB, 2006; 14 days; budget=8€/day). The north was off limits, and maybe will one day be a hot-spot for traveling.

Places
Kampala - Jinja - Masindi - Murchison Falls NP - Ssese Islands - Kabale - Bognyonyi Walk - Kisoro - Mahinga - Mahingha Hike

Portugal

The capital, Lisbon, I visited for work, nice atmosphere every evening (AUG, 2006; 7 days), the north from Porto east to north was very nice too (JUN, 2018; 5 days).

Places
Lisbon - Porto - Lamego - Braganca - Montesinho - Moimenta - Campo Do Geres - Guimaraes

Morocco

Surprisingly nice given the many negative comments that I heard, Morocco shows its rich history, natural beauty and great food. The first visit only lasted for two weeks (NOV, 2007; 14 days; budget=14€/day) and I went back for another two (OCT, 2019; 13 days; budget=12€/day) for hiking in the northern Rif mountains, the central high atlas and the southern anti-atlas. Rural Morocco is interesting in its own right.

Places
Rabat - Casablanca - Fes - Marrakesh - Essaouira - Ouarzazate - Taroudant - Tiznit - Tafraoute - Walk Tafraoute - Dakhla - Tanger - Chefchaouen - Akchour - Azilal - Ait Bouguemez - MGoun - Kalaat MGouna - Tamellalt - Tinghir - Ait Benhaddou

Mauritania

Desert... dirty desert... No wonder a highlight in this country is the train ride with the longest train in the world right through the desert, as would be the camel trek with visit to the oases. It is a very depressing but somewhat interesting country (DEC, 2007; 10 days; budget=9€/day).

Places
Nouakchott - Nouadibou - Atar - Cinguetti

Senegal

While the first visit was only on transit, I went back to this slightly more upmarket country with nice people (DEC, 2007; 4 days; & NOV, 2018; 10 days, budget=14€/day). On transit I ended up stuck in peoples homes during Tabaski, interesting.

Places
St. Louis - Touba - Kaolack - Tambacounda - Dakar - Toubakouta - Popenguine - Ziguinchor - Oussouye - Cap Skirring - Diembereng - Kachiouane - Saly - Lac Rose

Mali

Touristed as hell, but if you take the time and play the game it is a very nice country with real tourist attractions, even if some are only a name like Timbuktu (JAN, 2008; 14 days; budget=7€/day). Travel can go really slow in these parts. I might cross it again one day.

Places
Bamako - Kayes - Sevare - Mopti - Kouriome - Timbuktu - Bandiagara - Dogon Valley - Hike Dogon Valley

Burkina Faso

Most people enjoy the friendly attitude of the Burkinabee while struggling to survive (JAN, 2008; 7 days; budget=12€/day). Do not go there for its tourist attractions. There are some places of interest though, if you have the time and a bit of extra cash.

Places
Ouagadougou - Ouahigouya - Bobo-Dioulasso - Banfora - Tengrela - Po

Ghana

A friendly lot... with a few national parks, beach and history to keep you busy (JAN, 2008; 17 days; budget=12€/day), just hanging out. It is cheaper than its surrounding countries and has arguably more to offer, and is also just very nice. I saw it referred to as Africa for beginners, I understand.

Places
Accra - Kokrobitey - Kakum NP - Cape Coast - Princess Town - Kumasi - Larabanga - Mole NP - Tamale - Bolgatanga - Tongo - Hohoe - Wii waterfall - Akosombo - Yeyi

Spain

A brief visit here (AUG, 2008; 7 days) was part of work going to a congress on statistics. The tourist part before and after was refreshing. And Andalusia (DEC, 2015; 5 days) just confirmed that Spain is a nice place to visit.

Places
Madrid - Barcelona - Oviedo - Granada - Ronda - Sevilla

Hungary

I liked it here (OCT, 2008; 7 days; budget=20€/day), but aside from beautiful Budapest there is not so much in terms of tourist attractions. Budapest was worth a second visit (SEP 2018; 1 day).

Places
Budapest - Eger - Pecs - Szeged

Republic of Serbia

Serbia has a little bit of a bad reputation but I enjoyed the vibe out there (OCT, 2008; 4 days; budget=20€/day). The tourist information offices were the sweetest too. I did go back, for a road trip, east-west and south (AUG 2018; 5 days)

Places
Belgrade - Novi Sad - Subotica - Nis - Golubac - Negotin - Studenica Monastary - Novi Pazar - Nova Varos - Uvac Meanders - Ivanjica - Lazarevac

Bosnia and Herzegovina

A first time I crossed into Bosnia just to visit nice Sarajevo (OCT, 2008; 3 days; budget=20€/day). A second time I visited other parts of Bosnia and Herzegovina (JUN, 2014; 3 days; rock bottom=6€/day). The people you end up talking to are very nice, but many tourist attractions you will not find.

Places
Sarajevo - Kostajnica - Bosanska Krupa - Sanski Most - Jajce - Mostar - Blagaj - Procitelj

Bulgaria

While there is not so much in terms of tourist attractions also Bulgaria was OK for a visit (OCT, 2008; 7 days; budget=20€/day).

Places
Sofia - Plovdiv - Veliko Tarnuvo - Rila Monastery - Bansko

Macedonia

North Macedonia is yet another Balkan country that is nice but that lacks tourist attractions. Some national parks are surely worth a visit (OCT, 2008; 2 days and JUN, 2014; 3 days; budget=17€/day and AUG, 2018l 1 day).

Places
Skopje - Bitola - Ohrid - Mavrovo - Galichnik - Janche - Debar

Kosovo

Kosovo is not even considered a country by some, but the borders are there. I just went in for a quick experience (OCT, 2008; 2 days). It felt a bit like visiting Africa, so much infrastructure is simply lacking. It seemed much improved the second time, when I enjoyed the north for hiking (AUG 2018; 2 days).

Places
Pristina - Prizren - Peja - Accursed Mountains

Mexico

Air-con buses and expensive cars, a bit too smooth for me (DEC, 2009; 16 days; budget=14€/day). A bit too smooth for me, but I enjoyed the friendly people, rich history, natural scenery and food. I focused on the southern provinces: slightly boring Yucatan and much nicer Chiapas.

Places
Mexico City - Taxco - Grutas de Cacahuamilpa - Cuernavaca - Cancun - Valladolid - Chitzen Itza - Merida - Cenotes Cuzama - Campeche - Palenque - Yaxchil?- Bonampak - San Cristobal - Sumidero Canyon

Cuba

Interesting, rather than nice (DEC, 2009; 15 days; budget=20€/day) with quite boring cities and some difficulty to get off the tourist track. For me the highlight was a huge outdoor party because it was so surreal. People at the time did not address you out of fear for the police.

Places
Havana - Vinales - Trinidad - Sanctu Spiritu - Santiago de Cuba - Baracoa - Santa Clara - Remedios

Guatemala

Guatemala is deservedly on the tourist radar (JAN, 2010; 14 days; budget=10€/day), for partying, hikes up volcanoes and in the mountains, cultural history and diversity with indigenous people around. Moving around can be fun in the chicken buses, and contact with people in general is good despite the many tourists.

Places
Guate - Panajachel - San Pedro La Laguna - Antigua - Xela - Chichicastenango - Santa Cruz de Quiche - Nebaj - Coban - Lanquin - Semuc Champey - Poptun - El Remate - Flores - Tikal - Rio Dulce - Livingston - Monterrico

Honduras

In transit (FEB, 2010; 3 days; budget=10€/day). It is known to party people and for cheap diving and gang violence. It appeared maybe a bit boring. I did not visit the capital Tegucigalpa.

Places
San Pedro Sula - Comayagua - La Esperanza - Gracias - Choluteca - Copan Ruinas - Celaque NP - Tela - La Ceiba - Utila - Trujillo - Tegucigalpa

El Salvador

Lacking tourist attractions, you might end up here for friendly interaction with locals and super friendly tourist information officers (FEB, 2010; 7 days; budget=14€/day). For surfers it might be good here too, not for swimming.

Places
