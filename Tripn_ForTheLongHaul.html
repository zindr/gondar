South East Asia loop (2001)

malaysia - indonesia - thailand - laos - vietnam - cambodia

[ 85 days ]

First solo trip, going back to Indonesia and doing the loop that many were doing, especially with Cambodia opening up and with Vietnam that was already very high on my list. The trip was a series of first experiences, intense, freedom in its purest sense. With the beauty of the jungle, the adventurous traveling, brilliant food, but also with the poverty, basic life and transportation, the culture shocks, it took quite some time to digest.

Indonesia I knew was going to be great, with its fun people, and jungle hikes. Cambodia, with its superb Angkor Wat and wild atmosphere at the time, together with intense Vietnam, they were really my taste, cycling through exquisite Hanoi, floating around in the Mekong Delta, trying to get the best deals. Thailand, and even more so, Malaysia, at the time, were fun, but primarily passing through countries, they felt a bit too easy.

South Asia crossing (2002)

turkey - iran - pakistan - india - nepal - bangladesh - myanmar

[ 112 days ]

Any trip lures you back, at least to nearby places that you just missed out on. So, following a suggestion of a fellow traveler, why not start from the east of Turkey and travel to Myanmar, bordering Thailand. Still fueled with energy of the previous trip, maybe also way too naive, thinking that I could arrange for a visa to get in Syria, and to arrange other visas on the road without a ticket back.

Going to Iran was exciting and very very interesting, although I did not like it that much, and it was only in Pakistan that I got my real traveling buzz, just traveling through it. It was definitely a much more rewarding environment than India which appeared overcrowded with uninteresting tourists and annoying locals, even so much that I felt weight falling of of my shoulders when entering Nepal. Funny, coming back from tourist free Bangladesh into India I seemingly felt the same, but now comming into India. Myanmar, that was something else at the time, gradually opening up to tourists.

East Africa, from north (2005)

egypt - sudan - ethiopia - kenya - uganda

[ 62 days ]

Meeting other travelers is often the biggest incentive, to push it further, taking the next step in my chain of countries from Egypt to Uganda. And I admit, even though I had build some traveling experience in Asia, going to Africa was maybe a tiny bit scary. With countries on the list like Sudan, why would I have been a bit wary ?!

Sudan is not, and will never be, a tourist hub, but I am happy I got a taste of it, despite its depressing state. Ethiopia on the other hand ended up being an absolute highlight, with its superb hiking in the Simien, its culture and lively people, loved it, also the difficult and slow way of traveling through. The food, let's forget about that. More south, Kenia was definitely OK, Uganda I was lucky to run into some very fine experiences, with close contacts with locals, great stories. Africa turned out a real joy to travel in, just for being there.

West Africa, from north (2007)

morocco - mauritania - senegal - mali - burkina faso - ghana

[ 65 days ]

What was possible in the east of Africa, I could do in the west, just go south. I was not ready to further from Kenya south, afraid of the budget, not the people. But it would again be Africa, immersing myself in the African madness, a not to understand type of madness that I loved. Traveling in Africa more an more proved just trying to get from place A to B, really fun. This was french speaking country, with french tourists, here they are, some of them were nice.

After depressing Mauritania I was thoroughly relieved when entering Senegal, just to cross it to Mali which would be my highlight, with a solo hike through the Dogon country, a country to spend time making good deals, and the boat trip to Timbuktu, rough and real. But also Ghana offered very nice experiences, various things to see and do, much more so that Burkina Faso but that was definitely worth it too.

Central to South America (2009)

mexico - cuba - guatemala - honduras - el salvador - nicaragua - costa rica - panama - colombia - ecuador

[ 110 days ]

For the third time going south, but now in Central America right down to the equator. I wanted to visit Cuba before things would change with Fidel getting old. The biggest worry would be how to cross the gap from Panama to Colombia, but I would find a way. So, the timeline was very vague.

An up and down to Cuba was easy to arrange, had a very interesting time at Remedios Christmas party. The south of Mexico fun and with many interesting sites, Maya stuff. I did spend a week on a Spanish course moving into Guatemala, a very good decision for further travel, amazing country too, fun to travel through, all the way up to Tikal. Honduras I just traveled through, a bit more time in El Salvador where the tourist information offices turned out to be a highlight, fun and extremely helpful for fast paced travelers like myself. A favorite in this trip would be Nicaragua, not boasting loads of tourist stuff, but nice, especially Isla de Omoteppe, very nice. Costa Rica had the Corcovado, an absolute jungle experience, but otherwise I skipped most because of the budget, same with more boring Panama. Although, I would not want to have missed the San Blas Islands from which I also could take a boat to Colombia.

South America, I made it. Colombia was a bit more upmarket, so it seemed, I just enjoyed the 5 day Ciudad Perdida hike, and not to forget, lovely Quito in neighboring Ecuador. I actually went down there to reach the equator, the symbolism of it. The Americas, there is a lot to see and do but it is not Africa or Asia, most cities are a bit boring, but there is plenty to do outside the cities.

Southern Africa, up north (2010)

south africa - namibia - zimbabwe - zambia - malawi - tanzania

[ 55 days ]

Bought a 20 euro one person tent to beat the budget, let's go for it I thought, finish the next leg of my chain of countries in Africa. There were very few destinations I had in mind, just to cross it and make my chain. This trip made it clear, just go, it will be good.

South Africa was just for entry, to Namibia. Was I lucky there, met a guy with a car, crossed the whole country with him, we were the first 2 wheel drive ever to make it all the way up, thanks to the new road. Victoria falls in Zimbabwe, -must see- when around, was unlike the rest of Zimbabwe which shows various extremes, had much fun with a guide I just hung out with. Zambia, that one I really loved, mainly because of me pushing myself and my wallet which led me from one big story into the next, absolutely brilliant, not everywhere you end up serving food at a company party, and sleeping in the police's court yard. It was absolute freedom, sleeping next to the truck you hitchhiked with, being stuck in tiny villages waiting for anything to pass by. From Malawi onward, it was still nice, but also a bit more normal, although, in Africa it is never fully normal. No real highlights, but just fun to enjoy the continent.

South America (2011)

peru - bolivia - paraguay - argentina - chile - brazil

[ 87 days ]

Again, went all the way south, but this time in the America's, starting from Peru to chain up with Ecuador. Bolivia would be the main country, and I would cross it with my girlfriend, but otherwise, aside from Iguasu, it was just wait and see. Amazing to realize how little I know before crossing a continent. And I made it, down, and back up again, to Rio de Janeiro. A trip with foremost some natural highlights, because the cities, the food, the traveling is not the best out here, especially not in the south.

Peru was fun, also its people, and I did do the Machu Pichu like everybody else. Bolivia, there I did not like the people, at all, but the first two weeks were mind blowing, from one highlight into the next one, on and on. Paraguay, let's say, it is a weird place, very weird, not that I recommend it, but I am happy that I was there. Argentina has some nice places, just like Chile, I really loved Tores Del Paine, can you believe I only learned about its existence when talking to other tourists, immediately changed plans too. Lucky they torched the place the day I left and not 5 days earlier.

Central Asia loop (2012)

iran - turkmenistan - uzbekistan - kyrgyzstan - tajikistan

[ 54 days ]

Time to fill in some gaps, having covered east to west. It took me 6 weeks straight to arrange all of my visas to Central Asia. At the time, the main goal was Turkmenistan, because it would be weird, and Uzbekistan, because of Samarkand, they were the desert part. Then came the mountain part, I loved the mountains.

I first flew into Iran, just for a few days, a country that partly was how I remembered it, but less oppressive this time. Independently crossing Turkmenistan, that meant 5 days max at the time, weird, but also normal'ish. Uzbekistan has more to offer, but is over-touristed, not very nice, what a difference with Tajikistan, waaw, with it's Pamir Highway, pure adventure, not easy but very very rewarding. Kyrgyzstan were I met up with my girlfriend was a bit the same, but more easy, the highlight was the Ala Kol lake. Oh yeah, the 3 day layover in Moscow was absolutely worth it too.

South East Asia islands (2013)

indonesia - malaysia - japan - brunei - singapore - philippines

[ 77 days ]

Filling in some more gaps, and with the Philippines in mind, I had a good excuse to visit the eastern part of Indonesia, and Japan. But when around, the other countries made sense. It was definitely an island hopping trip, very much unlike earlier trips, with 9 local flights, not proud of that.

Indonesia is always fun, had a great time with the people, did a trip with some locals to Rammang Rammang, and a lot of island hopping by overnight ferry. Japan turned out to be awesome too, but mainly because I was very lucky with the people I met. This trip couchsurfing made a real difference. In the Philippines I especially liked the hike in Banaue, and the snorkeling in Palawan. While Malaysia had its moments, it was Singapore that was most appealing, and Brunei, weird, weird and small and boring, weird.

Gulf to South Asia (2014)

uae - oman - india - sri lanka - qatar

[ 52 days ]

A ticket to Sri Lanka, up and down, costed about the same as one to the UAE, from Oman to India, up and down to Sri Lanka, and back through Qatar and home, so that is what I did. While expecting the most from Sri Lanka, it was Oman that ended up being most memorable, and India that showed that it could be a fun country to travel disproving my earlier experiences.

The UAE and Qatar are not backpack friendly, but that maybe pushed me to the right places, loved the desert party I ended up, and to see plastic fantastic, why not. Also Oman is not backpack friendly, unless that is all you need, a backpack, it ended up my cheapest country ever, 3 dollars a day, with superb hiking. Sri Lanka was ok, not too many attractions, same for India only that makes up with it colorful life and temples, it was fun to be there. Hampi was surely a success, the backwaters were alright too.

Central America, again (2017)

guatemala - belize - honduras - nicaragua - costa rica - el salvador

[ 37 days ]

Why did I not go diving in Honduras the previous time, so let's go there now. And while I am at it, it would be the opportunity to do those things that I left out the previous time, like Belize, and Costa Rica that I quickly crossed.

The diving was a great experience, and I did some great hiking, an activity I increasingly spent time on, and happy to finally spent some time in Costa Rica as well as I crossed it way to quickly the first time. Also in El Salvador I did the extra hike, and had fun in the local pub. In any case, I largely got what I came for, I had been there before and I knew what I would get. Also loved my one day layover in sunny New York.

South America, west side (2019)

ecuador - peru

[ 42 days ]

With hiking being ever more prominent in our trips, the Huayhuash seemed an suitable endeavor, and if I was to fly back to Peru, why not fill in the gaps in the north that I left out before, and even more up in Ecuador where I had only been 5 days in 2010.

Various hiking trips made a real difference, in Ecuador the marvelous Cajas was the main event, in Peru the out of the way Gran Vilaya was a super intro, in the center there were the starter hike Laguna 69 and cherry on top, Huayhuash, 8 days of ultimate high. Also aside from hiking these countries are worth the time and effort, but now we hiked.
