South East Asia loop (2001)

My first solo trip. I was in Indonesia before, that gave me taste to come back and with Cambodia being increasingly open for traveling it made sense to do the whole South East Asia loop.

The first few days in Malaysia did not really exite me that much. Sumatra in Indonesia where I spent two weeks was much more my thing, with as highlight the trek in Leusser National park and a few hikes near Berestagi. The world trade center bombing was one of the reasons almost no tourists were around.

In Thailand I felt that things were just a bit too easy to enjoy, with loads of tourists and locals that did not care anymore about them. I was nice, but only that. I did not fancy sitting on the beach to drink beer and chase women, maybe I should have.

Laos was really relaxed and on my own not that interesting, locals did not interact much and I was surrounded by groups of Israelis most of the time making me feel even more lonely.

Vietnam with its inquisitive and in your face kinda people made it all very different, here I really got into traveling and I learned about what I thought was most important. Hanoi at the time was just brilliant, and in the south the Mekong Delta was amazing too.

Cambodia did what Vietnam did, just opening up to tourist after years of turmoil. The people were so enjoyable although not always easy. Phnom Penh was the type of city for me, and Angor Wat simply mind blowing. Unfortunately, at the time, many places were still off limits or difficult and expensive to get to.

I finished in Thailand again, a few final days of shopping to end my first 3.5 month trip.

Accross Southern Asia (2002)

After my trip through some South East Asian countries I got the idea to go from Turkey, where I had been before, to Burma.

So, I booked a one way to Istanbul and went straight to the east were I had not been before. I was going to Syria, at least that was the plan, but then I met a Japanese guy who came overland from India which changed my plans. You should know that the Pakistani embassy told me that the border was closed with India and because of growing tensions following the bombing of Afghanistan the country was also adviced against for traveling. If he could, I can as well, and I skipped Syria and went straight for Iran.

Iran was a very interesting country and it made me feel like an adventurer again, but all in all I did not feel that great over there, maybe because it is a very grey country, maybe because people almost invariably asked me to help them get to Belgium. So I did not take too much time, only about two weeks, and went fairly quick to Pakistan, not knowing what to expect.

Pakistan was really the country for me, I did not go to the south because Karachi is a bit a dangerous place, but I went to many other places in the center and north. The highlights were the mountains in the north, really nice for hiking, and maybe the sufi dance performances in Lahore.

Then I crossed India, by most people considered as a hate it or love it country, I did neither, it was very interesting with a culture I had never encountered before, boasting amazing cities like Varanasi. But it was also enoying because of the tourist hasstle, it was allways there. Note that at the time I only went to the Northern part. It was a hectic place, and I got fed up with it quite quickly, a burden fell of my shoulder when I entered Nepal.

I did not have too much time, with the stuff that I wanted to do before new years, so I decided not to go trekking in Nepal, like the Anapurna circuit, maybe I should have. Kathmandu was an absolute highlight over there, and maybe even the Chitwan National Park turned out special. But, I quickly moved on, back into India because I still had to arrange for a visa and flight ticket into Myanmar wich proved to be quite difficult.

I finally managed in Calcutta and while waiting for the flight I went in and out of Bangladesh, the country without tourists. It was hard, not knowing anything in a country that is not used to dealing with tourists. But, maybe that is why I look back at it with great pleasure, it was fun just being there, well, fun, maybe it was more interesting instead, but hectic as hell so that even India felt a bit relaxed afterwards.

A flight took me into Myanmar and that was again an amazing country, with people that were to nice and a culture that was quite different. I really loved it, Yangon was superb and Hsipaw a bit more north, also Thaton and Hpa An because of the lovely people and spend all the days on my visa, before I flew home. Another trip finished, I made a link of countries between Turkey and Indonesia, and an idea was born, to make a link that crosses the whole world.

Middle East (2004)

Ok, I made a link between Indonesia and Turkey, so now it was time to make it even further, and I set out to travel from Egypt to Turkey.

What to do in Egypt is clear, with its pyramids and even more amazing, the temples in Luxor like the one of Karnak.

I further moved into Jordan, which I did not like because of the tourist prices but admittedly it has three unmissable assets, Petra, the Wadi Rum desert and floating in the Dead Sea.

I was happy to enter into Syria, a place where most people were again very nice, only unfortunately, there was not that much of interest to tourist in this country. I enjoyed my time just hanging around, apparently, that is what I like.

It was just a small trip, I was going to start with my Phd. in Methodology of Educational Research, a different type of experience to look forward to.

North to East Africa (2005-6)

In Egypt I met a guy who crossed Africa with his motobike, and that changed a lot for me. The year after I was ready to do partly the same, go down from Egypt for a few months. This time in Egypt I was before, so I could rush through it, only stopping at Abu Simbel and Aswan.

The next stop, Sudan, I was in for an exiting time of going into Africa. In fact, it was amazing, but much more easy as expected, in Sudan the hospitality was great and I kinda regret not having spent a bit more time there, just for the interaction. It is nevertheless a country with a fair share of problems.

The next stop was Ethiopia, which eventually turned out to be one of the coolest places I have ever been. The interaction with the people is not always easy, but alive and kicking. The Simien Mountains were really great, chewing qat, so many things.

Kenya was the next stop, and after getting over the first fear of Nairobi it ended up being just a nice country, maybe nothing more. For me, it is the country where I did my safari.

Uganda was a bit more to my taste, with a very good experience in the Murchison Falls national park. I guess in Uganda, a bit more things were happening.

After Uganda, it was time to head back to Kenya and fly home, back to work. But surely, traveling in Africa was quite similar to traveling in Asia. I would be back.

North to West Africa (2007-8)

The south of Africa did not look so appealing, it would definetely be more expensive. So I figured, I made the link with Europe on the eastern side, lets do the same on the western side and go from Morocco all the way to Ghana.

I was not really looking forward to Morocco because I heared it was not a nice place to travel, but, that turned out different, I really loved it there, in almost all places, off course you get the tourist crap but you get so many other things as well. I especially like the city Fes and the mountains near Tafraoute. Note that to proceed south meant crossing the Western Sahara, debatably a part of Morocco

Mauritania is clearly another cup of tea. It is nice to do overlanding like me, I would normally never ever want to go this god forsaken country, but it was interesting to say the least. I was happy to leave after 10 days though, to gray and depressing, but a world of its own.

While not necessary for making the link, I crossed into Senegal, just briefly for convenience on my way to Mali. I was there during the Tabaski festivities that brought me into peoples homes. It was really interesting, but all in all Senegal was not really on my itinerary.

Mali was on my itinerary, where Timbuktu is. The Dogon Valley was an absolute highlight, contrary to popular belief, it is possible to do it by yourself, just talk to people. It is a hard bargain everywhere but still, the interaction with the people is rewarding.

Burkina Faso is a bit more quite, in many ways, but charming I guess. There is not so much to do, I loved the scenery in the far south. I did not go up north.

So far it was French speaking, but once in Ghana that changes to English, and a lot of things change, clearly Ghana is a bit different. It has a lot to offer, the Mole National Park for example, but also the slave trade history is on display. It is also a place to eat dog or bat. Africa for beginners, Ghana was referred to at that time.

After Ghana I just made it up north again, to Burkina, to catch my plane. Ones in Africa, it grows on you, while there is nothing, just being there often is rewarding as it is.

Central to South America (2009-10)

I wanted to visit Cuba, before things would change with Fidel getting old. With much time available it was clear that I could just combine that visit with the other countries in Central America.

The obvious place to start was Mexico, maybe not immediately the country of my strongest interest but it has Chitzen Itza so I head to go there. The traveling was a bit too expensive and too easy, but in the end, I admit that is was nice, especially the south west of the country.

Cuba on the other hand did not live up to my expectations, although I did like Havana a lot. Most of Cuba I thought was a bit boring, people on the street were not allowed to talk to me, every place looked a bit the same and it was almost impossible to get out of the tourist trail which was expensive. A highlight was probably the party at Remedios, where I partied and slept on the street.

The country with arguably most to offer is Guatemala. Tikal for example was impressive, and it was possible to see lava comming out of a crater too. The traveling was a bit rough, the way I like it. Some places were a bit messed up because of tourism. I finally took some Spanish classes which would help me throughout the rest of my trip.

Afterwards, I briefly went in to Honduras, a country that is famous for cheap diving, and for gang violence. I only went through it, it was alright.

The next stop was El Salvador, no idea why I went there, and even when I was there I still had no idea why I was. But hey, the atmosphere was good, and these days I am just hanging around in places anyway.

After passing Honduras again briefly, the next stop was Nicaragua. This is the country that I like most of this region, clearly a bit more poor than its neighbors but with a great vibe to it. The Isla de Omotepe was really a cool place, not in the least for the nice hike up the Reserva de Biosfera. I did not like the tourist towns, but the boat travel, and the different Bleufields area all contributed to my appreciation.

While I would never want to go to touristy Costa Rica, I simply had to, and I am happy I did. The Corcovado National Park turned out to be one of the highlights of my trip. The rest was also alright, but the effects of tourism was not always nice to deal with.

Panama I did not like that much, except for a jungle hike and the San Blas Islands that I passed on my way down to Colombia. I think the atmosphere was not that good, boring cities, boring everyting.

Going with a small boat to Colombia was a great starter. Colombia as a country did not really convince me, it is much more upmarket than I expected. There are a few interesting places, and although expensive and touristy I kinda liked my Lost City hike. I finally got to do a bungee jump, and a rappel, well... not completely my kind of country but interesting nevertheless.

Because I had a little bit of extra time I decided to just hop in and out of Ecuador to make it to the equator, a bit of a symbolic thing I guess. Quito was a highlight of my trip, and this was made even more so because of the equator where you can do a few nice tricks. And now I know rafting is boring, I did a number 4 but the only thing of interest was the jungle around.

In the end, I just returned to Colombia to fly out. The Americas, there is a lot to see and do but it is not Africa or Asia, most cities are a bit boring, but it was good nevertheless.

South to East Africa (2010-11)

The cheapest countries I went to, so it was time to just go for it and try to cross from where I left of in Africa, Kenya, to the south, South Africa. I knew some of the countries might not have been my taste, but I had to make the link anyway. I started in South Africa, in Cape Town, but it is a bit too white for me. I was happy to leave it quickly.

Namibia started out being too white as well, and Windhoek I considered the most boring capital that I have ever been. I chose to try hitch-hiking, and that turned out really well. The scenery is simply amazing, and I found a ride for a brilliant road trip for over a week. In the end I was happy to end up in the black part of Africa again.

Because I wanted to see the Victoria Falls, I went to Zimbabwe which had improved a lot since 2008 when it economically completely crashed. The falls were impressive, the rest of Zimbabwe was just nice to hang out, interesting place with interesting conversations.

To make my link I had to pass Zambia as well, and for me that meant spending at least a number of days there, not just passing through. I did not know what to do, but I did, and to my surprise this country ended up being one of the most unforgetable, with really cool things happening and a taste of Africa in its most African way, getting stuck and waiting for transport in the middle of nowhere, for days on end.

When I finally got out, I ended up in Malawi where I knew not much was happening, but for me it would have been ok to celebrate the new year. It is ok as a country, but I assume that it is more appropriate for people who like sitting on their ass at the lake or who like smoking weed all day.

The last stop, making the link, was Tanzania. Maybe a bit the same feeling as in Kenya, it just did not really work for me. The tourist industry made prices go up for safaris, and for the rest there is not that much of interest. The one clear exception was Zanzibar, I liked it a lot, just the atmosphere was so different there.

Looking back at my trip, I am most happy with Namibia for its natural scenery and Zambia for its way of traveling. These countries made a difference, and in the end I made my link from the south upwards to Europe and to Indonesia.

South America (2011-12)

Making links, the possibilities for large overland trips are gradually decreasing. Maybe the Americas did not fully convince me, but I had to cross from Ecuador further down in South America to also make it to the edges in this continent.

Peru was the obvious choice, with its steeply priced Machu Pichu. I especially enjoyed some of the interactions with the people which was surprisingly nice given the many tourists that visit the country.

The country that is recommended most, in South America, that is Bolivia and I understand why. The Salar was without a doubt spectacular, the Pampas very nice, and the Choro trek highly enjoyable. But here I did not like the people, some were nice but most of the people I met seemed to lack all forms of mindfulness and empathy.

A country that is not recommended, but also I undestand why is Paraguay. First we had to cross the Chaco, an impressive stress of nothingness. Maybe there are no tourist attractions, but things can be a bit weird here and a bit crazy, I loved it.

The next stage was Argentina, unfortunately again very expensive, for us but also for the locals. It was really hard to keep my budget, and hitch-hiking as a guy was difficult. The highlight was clearly Patagonia, and Buenos Aires. The Iguasu Falls also were impressive. The cities in general were dull and uninspiring.

I learned from another tourist that Torres del Paine was so impressive to I decided to hop into Chile as well. And not only was Torres del Paine absolutely great, the vibe in the town was nice too. This lead me to spent a bit more time in Chile, with a vibe that I liked more than in its neighbor. It is also very expensive though, hard to keep my budget.

I had to cross through Bolivia again, which was a pain with yet another road block that costed me 5 days, asshole Bolivians, and Paraguay where I got stuck in the mud which costed me an extra day. Finally I ended up in Rio de Janeiro, which was really great, happy to have been there to end my time in South America.

Central Asia loop (2012)

Having covered east to west, it was now time to fill up some gaps. There were a few countries that did attract my interest, and so this trip was build around one of them, Turkmenistan. I first flew into Iran, just for a few days, a country that partly was how I remembered it, but less oppressive this time.

I got 5 days of independent traveling in Turkmenistan, that was what I could get. It was weird, but not completely. It is maybe not the best place for backpacking because why would you go there, well... to experience how weird it gets.

Just north I entered in Uzbekistan which is famous for Samarkand. For me the country just did not do it, maybe because also here it is a bit gray and lacking diversity. I do not know, I admit, some parts were interesting.

Its neighbor Tajikistan was much more my thing, the interaction with the people and of course, the absolute highlight, the Pamir highway, a natural beauty high up in the mountains. I also like the roughness of the country, it is not so easy getting aroudn.

Traveling is much more easy in Kyrgyzstan where there is also superb mountain scenery, and a hike up to snow capped mountains was also one of the highlights. Just driving through it in certain areas is a tourist attraction in itself.

At the end, it was time to go home, making a three day transit in Moscow. Well, Moscow is very nice to be, you know most of the places, it speaks to the imagination. And then we were home again.

South East Asia islands (2013)

In a way I had my link across the world now, but I could add just a bit on the eastern side, in South East Asia by taking in a number of islands that I left out before because of the price.

The eastern part of Indonesia I wanted to visit already a long time, this was the moment, and I saw a different part of the country with much fewer tourists, again I loved it. I almost got lost in the jungle of Sulawesi, the hiking there was really nice. I had some really nice interactions with people in Flores and Western Timor as well. Few tourist attractions though.

Because I had to meet up with my girlfriend later, I first set out to Japan which was not as strange as I thought it would be, it is a bit strange, do not get me wrong. The food was brilliant and I had some really nice encounters.

From one strange country to another, flying into Brunei Darasalaam. This tiny place has not much to offer, maybe a daytrip would suffice but I did my best to stay just a bit longer. One of the mosques was simply unbelievable.

The eastern part of Malaysia was next, and this is not so much a country for me. I admit, Kuala Lumpur was good, but the rest of the country just seems to be a bit of an upmarket version of Indonesia that I loved much more.

Singapore on the other hand, definitely more upmarket, I did love a lot. The diversity it offered, mixtures of buildings and areas, the grandeur of the harbor area. I think I could live there if it were not that expensive.

Finally, we flew to the Philippines, a lovely set of islands. The northern rice fields were really great for hiking, and the northern stretch of Palawan were very cool for snorkeling. The rest of the country was simply very nice for hanging around and getting to places.

And so, we took a flight to Beijing for a last day and that was the end of another trip.

Madagascar (2013)

Madagascar is expensive to get to, but it speaks to the imagination. So long time ago I thought if I would ever marry, that would be my honeymoon. Well, I did not marry and I had a bit more money, so I figured, lets go for it.

It is Africa, despite what some say, but it is also that tiny bit different. I loved it, one of my favorite countries in Africa for sure. Even the food was kinda ok. There are also a lot of national parks, unfortunately only few can be reached with public transport. But the vibe is good, so I enjoyed just hanging around for most of the time.

Gulf to South Asia (2014)

I needed a place where I had not been yet, but could go in winter and that is why I ended up in the Gulf and nearby South Asia.

On my way to Oman I first flew into the United Arab Emirates. It is not a backpacking place for sure, it is made for cars and rich people. So it was very hard to keep my budget, and rough because of it, but ok, I did see Dubai and a few other places in the UAE, it was interesting, not fun.

But Oman was not completely different, only there is one thing to do, there are mountains, and you can hike and hike and hike. That is what I did, so I had a really good time. It is in a way also not a backpacking country, but as a tourist you can hitchhike around and sleep in the mountains, so it gets dirt cheap. The cities are boring, for most.

For the next country I was in for a surprise, because the south of India was not nearly as annoying as I remembered from my trip in the north. Ok, it did not have that many tourist attractions either, but it was fun to be there. Hampi was surely a success, the backwaters were alright too.

Sri Lanka was a bit the same as the south of India, but unfortunately, the prices for tourist attractions went up dramatically so I just did not do much over there. When in the neigbhorhood, maybe just stick to India.

My final stop was Qatar, a bit like Dubai and not a place for backpacking. Here I was extremely lucky to end up couchsurfing with the right kinda people. With one I went out with some expats which was nice, with the other I did a desert trip, ending with a desert party, covering half of the country, which was interesting too.

China (2014)

A short transit visit in China is not the same as really visiting it. So when I needed a country I could go to in autumn, the decision was made. I went back going all the way from east to west and north to south. It was nice, nothing too special given that it is such a big country though. I liked the people in general.

Return to West Africa (2015)

With only a few weeks to spare I made a short trip in West Africa again, partly because of the cheap airline ticket, visiting Togo and Benin. There is not so much to do but the people and the vibe are very nice.

Hop into Kazakhstan (2016)

With only a a week to spare I made a short trip to Kazakhstan, a country that I left out previous time in Central Asia but that has become much cheaper now. There is a reason so few tourist end up here, but still, it is also interesting.

Return to Southern Africa (2016)

Although I did not intend to ever go back to South Africa, combining it with Lesotho and Swaziland with a girlfriend it made sense. And I admit, it was a very nice trip, a highland road trip, hiking drakensberg, the safaris... the cities were dull, except for dangerous Jo'burg.

Return to Central America (2017)

The aim was to fill in the gaps, mainly on the Atlantic side, and that is what I did, too some extent. In general, it was an OK trip. Belize is different enough to warrant a small side trip. I am sure now that diving in Honduras is something to recommend, and even without it the visit was worth it because I made it too the other side of the country, although unfortunately not to Mosquitia because of the money involved. While in very nice Nicaragua there was nothing much to add to previous trip, just pointing out that it was normal that I loved its atmosphere, in beautiful Costa Rica there was a reason to go back. Costa Rica may only have its beautiful nature, but it does have plenty of that and it was good I went back. The rest was just on route, but it was fun socially in El Salvador, and even in Guatemala.
